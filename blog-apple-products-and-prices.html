Apple & Their Products - Are They Overpriced?

I don’t know how to start a blog (JK), but welcome to Cognosphere Blogs! Here at Cognosphere Blogs, we provide updates on our classes and talk about various tech topics from all over the world, from the latest tech updates to how they are applied in our day-to-day routine. Let’s dive right into the first ever blog!

When you hear the word “Apple,” what is the first thing you think of? For a lot of people (even myself), it’s their Apple products (iPhone, iMac, iPod, etc.) We just go into the Apple Store or a Network Provider and buy the iPhone which we will use for 6 months before the new one comes out, and we will do that same process again. But something that not many people understand is the amount of work that goes in to make an iPhone. Let me propose you a scenario:

You’re about to pay $699 on that new iPhone 11 Pro – or *audible gasp* $1,449 on the 256GB iPhone Pro Max without a trade-in. “That’s quite a few nights out on the town, you think to yourself, or as much as a used car. How much does it cost Apple to make these darn things?”

For a lot of people, they don’t want to go around learning about the iPhone but here is what’s shocking: You are paying so much more than you are supposed to. Each iPhone that Apple makes is just much cheaper so they can make profit. They use $490.50 while they make us seamlessly take out our credit card to pay the grand total off $699. For some of the skeptical people out there, you might be saying the rest of the cost goes to manufacturing, shipping and handling, and all that fun stuff, but to be honest, China does that for such a small fee, that Apple doesn’t need to worry about taking a loss - They make $8.46 on every iPhone made.

So, now you must be wondering, “has it always been like this? Were older phones cheaper, perhaps … or is Apple making more money off us today than it used to? We’re glad you asked. Let’s take a look at some earlier models.”

According to some detailed analysis, the 2018’s iPhone XS Max - the $1,250 phone with 256GB model only contains parts worth of $443. That just means that Apple is making significantly less money every time (not blaming politics) but prices of parts are beginning to soar into the markets. So, remember this: The next time you walk into an Apple Store, try to think if the product you are going to buy is going to be worth the money. And this is where technology comes in.
